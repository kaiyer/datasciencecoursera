User Manual

Introduction:

 

The CNG Vehicle Investment Breakeven Calculator helps a user perform an historical analysis of the number of years required to earn back the initial investment in a Compressed Natural Gas (“CNG”) powered vehicle based on the lifetime fuel savings compared to owning an equivalent Diesel powered vehicle. The historical time series data is dynamically obtained from the U.S. Energy Information Administration web site.  The historical price of CNG is estimated based on historical natural gas price to industrial customers in $/MMBTU. This is converted to a Gasoline Gallon Equivalent (“GGE”) basis based on the energy value of natural gas.  Various add on costs for converting natural gas to CNG at a retail fueling station are assumed and a “pump” price of CNG in $/GGE is estimated. In order to perform an “apples-to-apples” comparison of the cost savings, this price is converted to a Diesel Gallon Equivalent (“DGE”) price. Assuming the price of a CNG powered vehicle, the annual mileage, fuel economy of the diesel vehicle, fuel economy of an equivalent CNG vehicle, and fuel prices, we compute the annual fuels costs for a Diesel and CNG powered vehicle and the associated savings for using CNG as the fuel.  Finally dividing the vehicle price by the fuel savings gives the investment breakeven in years.

 

Inputs:

Vehicle Related Inputs:

1.       Vehicle Price: The price of the vehicle in USD.

2.       Annual Mileage: The estimated annual driving distance covered by the vehicle in miles

3.       Fuel Economy (Diesel): The fuel economy of the vehicle powered by a Diesel Engine in Miles per Gallon.

4.       Fuel Economy (CNG): The fuel economy of the vehicle powered by a CNG Engine in Miles per Diesel Gallon Equivalent.

CNG Pricing Related Inputs:

1.       Pipeline/Gas Acq/Marketer Services: This is the add-on for transporting, contracting and delivery of natural gas to the CNG filling station in $/GGE.

2.       Electric Compression Costs: Electricity costs related to using compressors and other equipment at the filling station in $/GGE.

3.       Maintenance Costs: Ongoing maintenance costs for operating of the filling station in $/GGE.

4.       Capital Amortization: Amortization cost of the filling station capital expense over its useful life in $/GGE.

 

Outputs:

1.       Fuel Cost Graph: This graph shows the historical estimated fuel cost of CNG in $/DGE compared to $/Gal for Diesel for a given set of CNG Pricing Related Inputs used in the calculator.

2.       Breakeven Graph: This graph shows the historical investment breakeven in years for the given Vehicle Related Inputs and CNG Pricing Related Inputs.

3.       Data Table: This shows the monthly input and output data in the calculator.
